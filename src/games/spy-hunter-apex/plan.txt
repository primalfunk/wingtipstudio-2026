Phase R6 - Road Expansion: Divided Highway / Median Split System
Work orders covered:
- Work Order 12: Divided Highway / Median Split System

Goal:
Expand the road model from one unified four-lane surface into a layout system capable
of smoothly transitioning into separated two-lane carriageways with a central median,
without changing the core one-way arcade driving model.

Required foundation:
- RoadLayout object with unified and divided layouts.
- LaneAnchorGenerator that replaces static lane positions.
- Divided entry, divided highway, and divided exit segment types.
- Smooth widening/merging over several seconds, not abrupt snapping.
- MedianRenderer for grass, guardrail, concrete divider, shrubs, signs, and utility details.
- Clean continuous median collision strips.
- Spawn support for player_side, opposite_side, and both carriageway targets.
- At least three divided-road visual variants.

Median gameplay rule:
- The median is not a shoulder and not a passable route.
- Player cannot cross the median in the MVP.
- Civilian, enemy, support, and player vehicles that fully enter the median hazard zone
  are destroyed immediately.
- Half-entering the median should commit the vehicle to the hazard, similar to current
  road-exit destruction, so vehicles do not snag or jitter on the divider edge.
- Future crossover ramps may override this rule only inside explicit authorized
  crossover zones.

1. PROJECT GOAL

Build a retro top-down highway combat game inspired by the structure of classic Spy Hunter, but with a hidden counterintelligence layer.

The visible game is fast arcade driving:
- dodge traffic
- destroy enemy vehicles
- manage damage/fuel/ammo
- collect support from vans
- survive highway segments

The deeper game is information warfare:
- commands may be spoofed
- support may be bait
- roads may be compromised
- civilians may signal warnings
- enemies adapt to the player’s habits
- the player must learn what to trust through play

The game should look and feel relatively simple, even old-school. The sophistication should come from systemic behavior, not visual spectacle.

Core fantasy:
The player is not just a driver. They are an operative surviving a hostile information environment at 140 mph.

2. TECHNOLOGY

Use:
- HTML5
- JavaScript
- Phaser 3
- Vite or equivalent lightweight dev server
- Arcade Physics
- simple sprite/tile assets first
- localStorage for persistent options / discovered hints later

Do not use:
- complex 3D

- large framework overhead
- premature procedural generation

4. DESIGN PRINCIPLES

4.1 Keep the surface game readable

The player must always understand:
- where their car is
- what lane they are in
- what can collide with them
- what is dangerous
- what is a projectile
- what is a support opportunity

Even when the information layer becomes confusing, the driving game should remain mechanically legible.

4.2 Never over-explain the deception systems

Do not show:
- commandTrust: 42%
- supportTrust: compromised
- enemyAwareness: high
- playerBeliefModel: trusts vans

Instead, express those states through events:
- the wrong van appears
- a command leads into danger
- a civilian flashes headlights before an ambush
- a support route suddenly contains fewer civilians
- an extraction message arrives too early

4.3 Let players form theories

The player should think:
- “Are motorcycles scouts?”
- “Are road signs safer than radio commands?”
- “Are repair vans being spoofed?”
- “Does the enemy know I always take detours?”

The game should neither immediately confirm nor deny these theories.

4.4 Make deception fair

The game may lie, but it must not feel arbitrary.

A false signal should usually have at least one subtle tell:
- unusual phrasing
- bad timing
- missing authentication chirp
- appearing after enemy scout contact
- contradicted by civilian behavior
- route environment feels wrong
- support vehicle behaves differently

Players should eventually improve at reading the deception.

5. CORE GAME LOOP

Each run proceeds like this:

1. Spawn player on highway.
2. Scroll road downward to simulate forward motion.
3. Spawn civilian and enemy traffic.
4. Present occasional signals:
   - command messages
   - roadside signs
   - civilian warnings
   - support notices
5. Player makes driving/combat/trust decisions.
6. Hidden systems update:
   - trust
   - route compromise
   - enemy awareness
   - player tendencies
7. Consequences occur:
   - ambush
   - safe supply
   - decoy van
   - route split
   - false extraction
8. Player survives or is destroyed.
9. Debrief gives cryptic operational summary.

Responsibilities:
- title
- start run
- controls
- optional “classified archive” later
- settings

Keep menu minimal and moody.

6.4 BriefingScene

Responsibilities:
- short mission framing
- no mechanical over-explanation
- launch GameScene

Example:
“Maintain northbound route. Recover support where available. Treat unauthorized transmissions as hostile.”

6.5 GameScene

Responsibilities:
- instantiate all runtime systems
- manage update loop
- own world objects
- coordinate collisions
- update UI

Most gameplay work happens here.

6.6 DebriefScene

Responsibilities:
- display run result
- score
- distance
- enemies destroyed
- support contacts
- suspicious signals
- cryptic intelligence notes

Do not fully expose hidden variables. Hint at them.

Example notes:
- “Two command packets failed authentication.”
- “Support convoy behavior inconsistent with known protocol.”
- “Civilian warnings correlated with later hostile contact.”
- “Enemy appeared to anticipate detour preference.”

6.7 GameOverScene

May be merged with DebriefScene initially.

8. PLAYER CAR

8.1 Movement

Use Arcade Physics.

Controls:
- left/right steering
- accelerate/brake if desired
- fire weapon
- optional special weapon later

Recommended MVP movement:
- constant upward travel illusion via scrolling world
- player moves left/right and slightly up/down within screen bounds
- no full free-roaming
- lane-biased steering but not locked lanes

Important:
The car should feel responsive, not realistic.

8.2 Player stats

Track:
- damage
- fuel
- ammo
- weapon level
- temporary effects
- current disguise/compromise flags later

8.3 Damage

Damage sources:
- collisions
- enemy bullets
- road hazards
- ramming enemies
- failed support contact
- ambush zones

Use generous early damage tuning. The deception layer is the novelty; the driving should not be punishing too early.

9.3 Enemy Vehicles

Types:
- Rammer: tries to collide
- Shooter: aligns and fires
- Cutter: moves laterally to block
- Scout motorcycle: observes player, may increase enemyAwareness
- Decoy civilian: appears civilian until close or triggered

9.4 Support Vehicles

Types:
- Weapons Van
- Repair Van
- Fuel Van
- Decoy Van

Support contact:
- player aligns behind or beside van
- van opens
- pickup occurs
- benefit or trap is applied

Support may be real, compromised, or decoy.

- river-approach
- extraction-candidate

10.3 Route Forks

Later Phase 3 feature.

At route forks:
- player chooses left/right/center route
- signals may recommend one
- each route has hidden tags
- consequences occur downstream

Important:
Do not require complex map generation. A route fork can simply choose the next segment profile.

11. TRAFFIC SYSTEM

TrafficSystem responsibilities:
- spawn civilian cars
- spawn ambient vehicles
- manage lane distribution
- prevent impossible overlaps
- despawn offscreen vehicles
- regulate density

Inputs:
- current road segment
- mission pressure
- route tags
- enemyAwareness
- signalNoise

Outputs:
- vehicle spawns

MVP rules:
- no spawn directly on top of player
- avoid unavoidable walls of traffic
- enemies introduced gradually
- civilian density gives route “texture”

12. SPAWN DIRECTOR

SpawnDirector decides what appears and when.

It should consider:
- elapsed time
- segment tags
- player performance
- trust/deception state
- enemy awareness
- pending consequences

Example:
If player followed a spoofed command, SpawnDirector schedules an ambush in 8 seconds.

Use delayed event scheduling:
- scheduleEvent(type, delayMs, payload)
- update checks when event should fire

This is critical for making deception feel causal instead of random.

13. COMBAT SYSTEM

MVP:
- player fires forward bullets
- bullets destroy enemies
- civilians should not be casually destroyed without penalty
- enemies may fire or ram

Combat rules:
- civilian hits reduce score / increase heat / damage trust
- enemy kills increase score
- shooting support vans has consequences
- shooting decoys may be correct but risky

Later:
- oil slick
- smoke screen
- missiles
- side weapons
- limited ammo

14. SIGNAL SYSTEM

14.1 Purpose

The SignalSystem presents information to the player.

Signal types:
- radio command
- road sign
- civilian cue
- support notice
- unknown transmission
- emergency warning
- extraction marker

14.2 Signal object

Use data like:

{
  id,
  source,
  visibleText,
  truthState,
  confidence,
  urgency,
  targetRouteTag,
  consequenceTag,
  createdAt,
  expiresAt,
  tellType,
  authenticationState
}

truthState:
- true
- false
- partial
- stale
- bait

authenticationState:
- authenticated
- weak
- missing
- spoofed
- unknown

14.3 Display

RadioPanel:
- shows one message at a time
- short text
- optional source label
- occasional static/flicker
- no walls of text

AlertTicker:
- brief messages
- warnings
- support calls

Road signs:
- physical objects on roadside
- must be readable at speed
- short phrases only

14.4 Signal examples

True:
“COMMAND: Support van two miles ahead.”

False:
“COMMAND: Proceed right for extraction.”

Partial:
“SIGN: ROADWORK AHEAD” but roadwork exists with enemy cover.

Stale:
“SUPPORT: Repair unit deployed” but it already left or was destroyed.

Bait:
“UNKNOWN: Clear route left” leading to low-traffic ambush.

15. TRUST SYSTEM

15.1 Purpose

Tracks reliability of information sources over the run.

Variables:
- commandTrust
- supportTrust
- roadSignTrust
- civilianSignalReliability
- unknownSignalDanger
- routeCompromise

These are internal only.

15.2 Updates

Trust changes based on:
- enemy spoof events
- player choices
- actual outcomes
- scout presence
- route compromise
- support success/failure

Example:
If motorcycle scout remains alive for 10 seconds:
- enemyAwareness increases
- probability of spoofed command increases
- routeCompromise may rise

15.3 Important design note

Trust is not a morality meter. It is operational state.

Command can be trustworthy early and compromised later.

16. DECEPTION DIRECTOR

16.1 Purpose

This is the heart of the game.

The DeceptionDirector decides when and how the information environment becomes hostile.

It should:
- choose deception opportunities
- avoid overusing deception
- preserve fairness
- adapt to player behavior
- create delayed consequences
- maintain uncertainty

16.2 Deception types

- false command
- decoy support
- compromised route
- fake extraction
- civilian mimic
- sign contradiction
- delayed ambush
- false safe zone
- real warning disguised as suspicious

16.3 Deception fairness

Every deception should have one or more tells.

Tell examples:
- missing authentication chirp
- message uses unusual wording
- arrives immediately after scout escaped
- road sign contradicts radio
- civilian traffic thins out
- support van drives too aggressively
- decoy van lacks escort pattern
- route has too many clean lanes

16.4 Deception pacing

Do not start with heavy deception.

Suggested run pacing:
- 0-60 seconds: mostly honest arcade game
- 60-120 seconds: first ambiguous signal
- 120-180 seconds: first meaningful falsehood
- 180+ seconds: adaptation to player habits

17. PLAYER BELIEF MODEL

17.1 Purpose

Infer player tendencies from behavior.

Track:
- obeysCommandSignals
- followsRoadSigns
- trustsSupportVans
- attacksSupportVans
- followsCivilianCues
- killsScoutsQuickly
- ignoresScouts
- favorsLeftRoute
- favorsRightRoute
- avoidsUnknownSignals
- shootsFirst
- preservesCivilians
- drivesAggressively

17.2 Implementation

Use counters and weighted scores.

Example:
When command recommends route and player takes it:
  obeysCommandSignals += 1

When command recommends route and player ignores it:
  disobeysCommandSignals += 1

Calculate tendency:
  commandCompliance = obeysCommandSignals / totalCommandRouteSignals

17.3 Enemy adaptation

If commandCompliance is high:
- increase command spoof chance
- make false command more rewarding-looking
- use fewer road sign deceptions

If supportTrust is high:
- spawn decoy vans
- fake support notices
- support ambushes

If player kills scouts quickly:
- scouts become rarer but more protected
- enemy uses passive road signs instead

If player ignores civilians:
- civilian warnings become more important but harder to distinguish

18. CONSEQUENCE SYSTEM

18.1 Purpose

Connect player choices to later outcomes.

Do not always punish immediately.

Examples:
- Following false command schedules ambush later.
- Accepting decoy support installs tracker.
- Ignoring real civilian warning increases roadblock chance.
- Killing real support reduces future support.
- Letting scout escape increases spoofing.

18.2 Event history

Track events for debrief:
- signal shown
- player response
- hidden truth
- consequence triggered
- outcome

This enables good post-run summaries.

19. UI / HUD

19.1 Main HUD

Show:
- score
- damage
- fuel
- ammo
- current weapon
- distance

Do not clutter.

19.2 Radio Panel

Small panel, probably top or side.

Requirements:
- readable text
- short messages
- source label
- subtle static animation
- message expiration

19.3 Alerts

Use sparingly:
- support nearby
- weapon armed
- damage critical
- route split
- extraction near

19.4 Debrief UI

Show:
- run score
- survival distance
- enemies destroyed
- civilian incidents
- support contacts
- signal anomalies
- operational notes

Do not reveal all hidden math.

20. AUDIO

Audio matters a lot.

MVP audio:
- engine loop
- weapon fire
- collision
- enemy destroyed

- signal static
- support pickup
- warning tone
- game over

Deception audio tells:
- authenticated command chirp
- missing chirp
- distorted chirp
- wrong static profile

This is an excellent subtle way to teach players.

23. INITIAL MVP DECEPTION EXAMPLES

23.1 False Command Ambush

Signal:
“COMMAND: Maintain right route. Support ahead.”

Hidden truth:
- spoofed after enemy scout escaped

Tell:
- no authentication chirp
- slight static distortion
- arrives soon after scout event

Player follows:
- road becomes empty
- then rammer pair spawns
- later support is absent

Player ignores:
- normal route continues
- possible real support appears later

23.2 Decoy Van

Signal:
“SUPPORT: Weapons van deployed.”

Hidden truth:
- decoy if supportTrust is compromised

Tell:
- van color slightly off
- opens late
- no escort beep
- drives too centered

Player docks:
- receives ammo but also tracker
- enemyAwareness increases
- later ambush chance rises

Player shoots:
- if decoy, reward
- if real, future supportTrust decreases

23.3 Civilian Warning

Cue:
- civilian flashes headlights twice and changes lane away from right side

Hidden truth:
- right side roadblock ahead

Player responds:
- avoids roadblock

Player ignores:
- roadblock spawns

Later twist:
- enemies may mimic this cue, but imperfectly

24. DIFFICULTY CURVE

Early:
- mostly honest
- low traffic
- obvious enemies
- real support

Middle:
- ambiguous signals
- scouts
- first false command
- route tags matter

Late:
- adaptive deception
- fake support
- contradictory signals
- route compromise
- stronger enemies

Difficulty should increase by:
- traffic density
- enemy coordination
- signal ambiguity
- support scarcity
- adaptation accuracy

Do not increase difficulty only by making cars faster.

25. DEBUG TOOLS

Create debug overlay toggle with a key like F1.

Show:
- current segment
- enemyAwareness
- commandTrust
- supportTrust
- routeCompromise
- active deception
- player tendency scores
- pending consequence events

This is for development only.

Normal players should never see it.

26. SAVE / PERSISTENCE

Later use localStorage for:
- best score
- longest distance
- discovered signal notes
- settings
- unlocked archive entries

Do not persist hidden trust from run to run at first. Each run should be self-contained.

Later campaign mode may support persistent enemy knowledge.

27. ART DIRECTION

Retro, readable, restrained.

Do:
- strong silhouettes
- clear lane markers
- readable vehicles
- limited palette
- subtle scanline/radio effects
- crisp UI

Do not:
- overdecorate the road
- obscure cars with effects
- use tiny unreadable text
- overload screen with lore
- make deception depend on imperceptible pixels

28. WRITING STYLE

Messages should be short, official, and slightly cold.

Good:
“COMMAND: Proceed north.”
“SUPPORT: Van deployed.”
“UNKNOWN: Left route clear.”
“WARNING: Signal mismatch.”
“SIGN: DETOUR AHEAD.”
“COMMAND: Ignore civilian interference.”

Bad:
“Hey agent, we think you should maybe go left because there might be a bad guy up ahead.”

Keep the writing terse.

29. SUCCESS CRITERIA

The prototype succeeds if:

- It is immediately playable as a top-down driving combat game.
- The player understands movement, collision, shooting, and support.
- The player begins noticing inconsistent information.
- At least one run creates a “wait, did the game lie to me?” moment.
- Deception is traceable after the fact in the debrief.
- The game feels deeper than it looks.

30. MOST IMPORTANT WARNING

Do not build the intelligence layer before the driving is fun.

The correct order is:

1. Fun driving.
2. Clear traffic/combat.
3. Useful support.
4. Signals.
5. Deception.
6. Adaptation.
7. Debrief.

The intelligence war only works if the arcade layer is strong enough to carry the player moment to moment.

31. NORTH STAR

The player should start by thinking:

“I am playing a retro car combat game.”

Then, after a few runs:

“I think command has been compromised.”

Then, after mastering the game:

“I survived because I understood what the enemy wanted me to believe.”

That is the game.
